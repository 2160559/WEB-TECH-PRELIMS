Animal Welfare

ADVOCACY


Dogs and cats are either spoiled (like a child who gets everything), treated as family, or they are empty/left alone, (treated or used in a very mean, unfair way) and, sometimes, killed for food. Strays are everywhere, we see them roaming the streets, living under cars and begging for scraps of food. Sometimes we hear reports of trucks of dogs on their way to slaughter, thankfully stopped (and looked at) by people in charge. The dog meat trade is alive and kicking here, (even though there is the existence of) a law (blocking or stopping someone or something) it. For one dog rescued, probably a hundred are killed.


Here are three ways you can help improve animal welfare in the city.


1. BEING A RESPONSIBLE OWNER


Having a pet is not only about fun and play. It comes with a lot of responsibility. When you get a pet, you are responsible for the life of that living creature, and it is your duty to feed and care for your pet for as long as they live. Make sure that you are capable of investing time and money for the well-being of your pet. The selection process matters too. Choose a pet that is well-suited to your (surrounding conditions). This means, you need to do some research before you go out and have a pet.


Aside from the pet’s compatibility with your home, family, location and schedule, you also have to make sure that the pet gets the complete preventive vaccines and proper training. That way, both you and your pet, will get the best out of your relationship.


2. KNOWING THE LAW


It is illegal to slaughter dogs or cats for meat and to mistreat them as specified in the Republic Act No. 8485, also known as the Animal Welfare Act of 1998. There is also a city ordinance that specifically bans the killing of dogs for livestock meat.


3. REPORTING ANIMAL ABUSE

If you find out about animal cruelty, or that your neighbours are planning to have asocena for their pulutan, report the incident immediately to your barangay officials.
